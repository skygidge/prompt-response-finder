# The Prompt Response — Style Guide

## Voice
Deadpan to mean. Informed. No winking. You're a person writing about things you've seen and tried, not a wire service filing copy. First person is allowed and encouraged — "I tried this," "photographed by yours truly," "Am I allowed to say that?" are all in voice.

## Headlines
- 2-3 words ideal
- Must contain a genuine double meaning that lands without explanation
- Both meanings must hold up under scrutiny
- No wordplay that requires footnotes

### Headline test (apply to every headline)
1. Does it have at least two meanings?
2. Do both meanings relate to the story?
3. Does it break down when you pull on it?
4. Would the reader get it without the blurb?

If a headline doesn't pass all four, use a straightforward sharp headline instead. A clean factual headline beats a forced pun every time.

### The first sentence extends the headline
The headline is the setup. The first sentence is the punchline, answer, or sharp entry point. Examples from published issues:

- **Cheap Shot** → "What's cheap, fast, and not my ex-girlfriend? This clever method to get placeholder product shots."
- **Pilot Episode** → "AI art generator aggregator (say it fast!) Higgsfield is trying its hand at short Internet series..."
- **Model Behaviour** → "AI imaging underdog Luma Labs launched agentic image generating..."
- **Unwatchable** → "A billion-dollar Disney deal couldn't save OpenAI's slop-fest Sora."

If your first sentence doesn't feel connected to your headline, one of them is wrong.

## Chinese headlines
- Default to a clean, sharp Chinese headline. Factual beats clever.
- Only use a pun if you are confident both meanings work in Chinese.
- English puns rarely translate. Don't force it.
- 模型 (AI model) and 模特 (fashion model) are completely different words. No overlap.
- 角色 (character/role) and 字符 (text character) share nothing.
- When in doubt, skip the pun.

### Examples of independent Chinese headlines (not translations)
- Cheap Shot → 一拍即合 (instant agreement + one shot/frame)
- Model Behaviour → "型"为 (model-like + behavior wordplay)
- Raising Concerns → 广告翻车 (ad disaster)
- Exit Strategy → 终止交易 (deal terminated)
- Penny Wise → 精打细"算" (calculating + compute)

The Chinese headline should be its own creative work. Start from the story, not from the English pun.

## Blurb rules
- First line: one sentence covering who/what/where/when/how. This is the news.
- Then bullet points (use `•`), maximum 7:
  - Each bullet is ONE sentence max. Fragments are fine: "15 seconds per image." "Unclear why."
  - At least one bullet noting something funny or ironic about the story — ONLY if genuine. Skip if nothing qualifies.
  - One bullet explaining why Insta360 employees would care (relevance to the camera/imaging industry, creative tools, design workflows, competitive landscape, etc.)
  - Remaining bullets: additional context, technical details, editorial opinion, whatever serves the story.
- Use `\n` between lines in JSON so they render as line breaks on the website.
- Lead with the sharpest fact or the headline's punchline
- End with a knife, not a summary
- Never invent product names. Everything in this newsletter is news. Every name must be real and verifiable.
- Do NOT change existing stories — this format applies only to new stories going forward.

### Structural templates
These patterns recur in the newsletter. Use them:

**In theory / In practice:**
> In theory, it means you create art with an AI-coworker. In practice, you get to watch a chatbot criticize its poor quality renders before spending more (of your) money to try and get it right.

**Setup / Subversion:**
> A billion-dollar Disney deal couldn't save OpenAI's slop-fest Sora. The Sora video generator app will stop working on April 26.

**Q&A:**
> What's an agent? Basically an AI that can do stuff, with Claude's "Cowork Mode" being a good example.

**Self-aware aside:**
> Hey, is this a newsletter or a Higgsfield advertisement? Alas, let's talk about the Higgs once again.

**Editorial punchline (last sentence only):**
> "This is why we can't have nice things."
> "So at least the safety filters work."
> "Whether it's proportionally smarter remains the industry's most expensive open question."

### What a good blurb does NOT do
- Dump technical specifications. If you're listing parameter counts, resolutions, and pricing in the same sentence, you're writing a press release.
- Read like Reuters. If a news wire could have written it, you didn't write it.
- Pad with context the reader doesn't need. Don't explain what a company does if the reader already knows.
- Repeat information from the headline in the blurb text.

## Links
- Links inline as short commands: "Read more." "Try it here." "Watch below."
- Always a complete sentence ending in a period.
- Never "You can find more information at..." or "For more details, visit..."

## Formatting
- English headline and blurb first, Chinese below
- Horizontal rule between stories

## What NOT to write
- No "This changes everything" hype
- No "X just dropped Y and it's insane" influencer voice
- No hedging ("It remains to be seen whether...")
- No corporate PR restatements
- No summaries that restate the headline
- No emoji. Ever.

## Good examples (from published issues)

> **Plus-sized Models**
> An LLM called Mythos is being released by Anthropic soon, according to an embarrassing leak. It's large, expensive and potentially dangerous if you believe the hype. Anthropic's models have never supported picture generation, so Nano Banana 2 still reigns supreme!

> **Penny Wise**
> The world's fourth most valuable company Microsoft just dropped the new MAI-Image-2 model. Boasting an alluring "price-to-performance" ratio, MAI-Image-2's demo does not yet work with reference images but did a decent job at rendering a homeless man's face. It also refused to generate an image of Bill Gates on Epstein Island, so at least the safety filters work. Try the MAI models here.

> **Unwatchable**
> A billion-dollar Disney deal couldn't save OpenAI's slop-fest Sora. The Sora video generator app will stop working on April 26. It turns out undeniably funny and sometimes racist AI videos won't be part of OpenAI's predicted trillion-dollar IPO.

> **Exit Strategy**
> Early-agentic-AI-darling Manus has been blocked from finishing a sale to Facebook-father-company Meta. The China-founded company agreed to a reported two-billion-dollar deal that would see the Manus founders and employees at its Singapore office doing Mark Zuckerberg's bidding. Not so fast! China's National Development and Reform Commission said, initiating a retrospective ban on the sale and reportedly barring Manus founders from leaving the Middle Kingdom. But the new Manus-feature is already visible on Facebook… awkward! Read more.

## Bad examples

> **AI Gets Bigger!**
> OpenAI is working on a really big new model! It could have over a trillion parameters, which is super impressive. This could change everything for the AI industry. We'll have to wait and see what happens!

> **New Model Alert**
> On April 21, OpenAI launched GPT Image 2, featuring O-series reasoning, native 2K resolution, up to 8 coherent images per prompt, and the ability to follow multi-turn editing instructions. The model replaces DALL-E 2 and DALL-E 3 APIs which shut down May 12. It supports 2048x2048 output and uses a reasoning-first architecture with 150 billion parameters.

## Chinese translation notes
- 提示 = prompt (AI), 回应 = response
- 又大、又贵 is punchier than 它很大、很贵
- [已屏蔽] is the Chinese equivalent of [redacted] for self-censorship jokes
- Match the casual register. Don't formalize.
